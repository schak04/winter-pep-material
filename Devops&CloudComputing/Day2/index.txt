## 1. What is Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC)?

SDLC (Software Development Life Cycle) is a structured process used to develop high-quality software in a systematic way.
It defines all the stages involved in software creation — from idea to maintenance.

### Phases of SDLC:

1. Requirement Gathering
2. Planning
3. Design
4. Development
5. Testing
6. Deployment
7. Maintenance

Imp:--->
SDLC ensures better quality, proper planning, cost control, and timely delivery.

## 2. Traditional vs Agile

### Traditional Model (Waterfall)

* Linear and sequential approach
* Each phase completed before moving to next
* Requirements fixed at beginning
* Heavy documentation
* Changes are difficult as it's impossible to move back and modify the changes.

### Agile Model

* Iterative and incremental approach
* Work divided into small cycles (sprints)
* Continuous customer feedback
* Flexible to requirement changes
* Faster delivery of working software

### Key Differences

| Feature              | Traditional | Agile     |
| -------------------- | ----------- | --------- |
| Approach             | Linear      | Iterative |
| Flexibility          | Low         | High      |
| Changes              | Difficult   | Easy      |
| Delivery             | One-time    | Frequent  |
| Customer Involvement | Low         | High      |
---

## 3. Problems in Traditional IT

1. Slow software delivery
2. Poor communication between teams
3. Late bug detection
4. Manual deployment process
5. High failure rate during releases
6. Long feedback cycles

Traditional IT separated development and operations, causing delays and conflicts.

Some other models are:-
(a) Spiral:-
The Spiral Model is a risk-driven software development model that combines elements of both Waterfall and Iterative models.
It focuses heavily on risk analysis and is best suited for large, complex, and high-risk projects.
The development process moves in a spiral (loop), and each loop represents a phase of the project.

Why it is called "Spiral"?
Because the development process is represented as a spiral diagram where each cycle (loop) goes through planning, risk analysis, development, and evaluation.
Each loop improves the product gradually.

Phases of the Spiral Model (Each Loop Contains):-

Planning:-
Define objectives
Identify requirement
Estimate cost and schedule
Risk Analysis
Identify potential risks
Analyze technical and business risks
Plan risk mitigation strategies
Engineering (Development & Testing)
Develop the feature
Perform testing

Evaluation:-
Customer reviews the progress
Feedback is collected
Decide next steps
Then the next spiral begins with improved requirements.

Key Features:-
Strong focus on risk management
Iterative development
Customer feedback at every phase
Suitable for large and critical systems

Advantages:-
Early identification of risks
Flexible to changes
Continuous customer involvement
Suitable for complex projects

Disadvantages:-
Expensive
Complex to manage
Not suitable for small projects
Requires risk analysis expertise
Best Suitable For
Large enterprise systems
Defense projects
Banking systems
High-budget and high-risk projects
